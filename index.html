Whop Transaction Forensics - Corrected Exposure Report

This report analyzes a high-ticket online education business selling courses and mentorship programs at $500-$9,500+ per sale through Whop.com. Customers pay via credit card (Visa, Mastercard, Amex) or buy-now-pay-later installment plans (Splitit, Affirm, Klarna). Splitit holds funds on the customer's own credit card and charges monthly installments. Affirm and Klarna pay the merchant upfront. This distinction matters because Splitit charges are much easier for customers or their banks to reverse.

What are disputes? A dispute (chargeback) happens when a customer contacts their bank or card company (Visa, Mastercard, Amex) to reverse a charge instead of requesting a refund. The bank pulls the money back and you must provide evidence to defend. The card network makes a binding decision - if they side with the customer, money is gone permanently with no appeal. Digital products are hard to defend because "delivery" is hard to prove.

Dispute Risk by Payment MethodTwo Different Problems

Card disputes and Splitit disputes are fundamentally different risks. Card disputes go through Visa/Mastercard/Amex directly. Their decision is final - if they side with the customer, money is gone permanently, often with a $15-25 fee. You have no recourse. Card disputes average $3,466 each and you lose 59% of them. Splitit disputes are smaller ($1,042 avg) but cascade into larger refunds.
